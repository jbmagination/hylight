Blues History

The Blues originated in Southern plantations. It was invented by slaves and ex-slaves, specifically african-american sharecroppers[1].
The Blues has a lot about self-pity and talking about how you feel[1].
Although created in the 19th century, it didn't spread out to the Midwest of America until 1930-1940[1].
Most of the original blues artists where murdered[1].
Blues origins are a mix of work songs, folk songs, spirituals and country blues[2].
Blues had a call-and-response feature like a lot of African music[2].
America was a rural country until the end of the 19th century. After the civil war the slaves brought the blues[3]
